Relative Pronouns: WHO, THAT, WHICH

Master the three essential relative pronouns to connect ideas and improve your writing

💡 What Are Relative Pronouns?

Relative pronouns are words that connect two clauses together and provide information about a noun. They help us add details without repeating words. The three main ones are:

WHO • THAT • WHICH

👤 WHO - For People Only

Use WHO when referring to people. It's the subject of the relative clause.

Example 1: "The teacher who teaches math is very experienced."
→ We're talking about a person (teacher)
Example 2: "I have a friend who loves to cook."
→ The friend is a person who loves cooking

📦 THAT - For People & Things (Essential Info)

Use THAT for both people and things. It introduces essential information that cannot be removed without changing the meaning. No commas used.

Example 1: "The car that I bought is reliable."
→ WHICH car? The one I bought. Essential information!
Example 2: "The student that won the scholarship is celebrating."
→ WHICH student? The one who won. Can't remove this info.

✨ WHICH - For Things Only (Extra Info)

Use WHICH for things, not people. It introduces extra information that's nice to know but not essential. Always use commas.

Example 1: "My laptop, which is very fast, helps me work efficiently."
→ Extra info with commas; we already know it's MY laptop
Example 2: "The book, which won a prize, became a bestseller."
→ Additional detail; "The book became a bestseller" works alone
